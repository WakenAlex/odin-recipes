Piftie

Description

Piftie, also known as "caltabos," is a traditional Romanian dish, similar to a meat jelly or brawn. It's made by simmering pork trotters, ears, and/or tails with aromatics like onion, garlic, and bay leaf until the meat is tender. After cooking, the meat is picked off the bones, chopped, and arranged in a mold. The strained broth is then poured over the meat and left to set in the refrigerator until it forms a jelly-like consistency. Once set, the piftie is unmolded, sliced, and served cold as an appetizer or part of a meal.

Ingredients

  • Pork trotters, ears, and/or tails
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Bay leaf
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Vinegar (optional)

Steps

  1. Clean meat thoroughly
  2. Boil meat briefly
  3. Drain and rinse
  4. Simmer meat with aromatics
  5. Cook until tender
  6. Strain broth
  7. Pick meat of bones
  8. Chop meat
  9. Arrange meat in mold
  10. Pour broth over meat
  11. Cool and refrigerate
  12. Unmold and slice
  13. Serve cold. Enjoy!
